HDFC Bank has recently revised its credit card terms, effective from August 1, 2024. Some of the key changes include:

1. Rental Transactions: A 1% fee will now apply to rental payments made through third-party apps like PayTM, CRED, and MobiKwik, capped at ₹3,000 per transaction.

2. Utility Transactions: For utility payments above ₹50,000, a 1% fee will be charged, also capped at ₹3,000 per transaction. Payments below this amount will not incur any additional fees.

3. Fuel Transactions: Purchases over ₹15,000 will now attract a 1% fee, with the same ₹3,000 cap.

4. Educational Payments: A 1% fee, capped at ₹3,000 per transaction, will apply to educational payments made via third-party apps. However, payments made directly through educational institutions' websites or POS machines, as well as international educational payments, are exempt from this fee.

5. International Transactions: A 3.5% markup fee will be imposed on all cross-currency transactions.

6. Rewards Redemption: Redeeming rewards points for statement credit or cashback will now cost ₹50, though this fee does not apply to certain premium cards.

7. Late Payment Fees: The late payment fee structure has been updated, with fees ranging from ₹100 to ₹1,300 based on the outstanding amount.


These changes are part of the bank’s effort to adjust its fee structures across various transaction types, impacting a wide range of customers
